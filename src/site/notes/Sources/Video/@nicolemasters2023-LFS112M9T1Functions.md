> The text discusses renal physiology, which involves the excretion and elimination of metabolic waste products, regulation of body fluid composition and volume, hormone production, and stabilisation of blood pH. The urinary system plays a vital role in maintaining these functions. Key organs include the kidneys, ureters, bladder, and urethra. Kidneys are responsible for filtering waste products from the blood plasma through nephrons, which generate urine. The urine is then stored temporarily in the bladder before being expelled from the body through the urethra.
